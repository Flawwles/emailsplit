Liquid Fuels Weekly Pricing Notification
08/02/2016
RM1013 Liquid Fuels
Framework Agreement
 
The weekly price calculation for orders placed against call off contracts under the Liquid Fuels Framework agreement RM1013 is as follows:
Total Pence per litre price = Commodity cost + Supplier Margin + CCS Management fee (£0.0015 or 0.15 0f a pence per litre).

The weekly commodity cost per product type is included on the attached spreadsheet and also shown below. Where CCS has undertaken a further competition on your behalf to award a call off contract under the framework, we have notified you of the contracted supplier and associated supplier margin. For Scottish customers where the further competition has been undertaken by Scottish Procurement, they will have notified you of the contracted supplier and associated supplier margin separately.
Assessment of
Fuel Supplies
 
Following a couple of issues and the FTA's (Freight Transport Association) recent report indicating a higher instance of illicit fuel in the UK market (see www.fueloilnews.co.uk) we wanted to highlight to customers that under the T&Cs of the call-off contract, providing it is done prior to delivery of the fuel, the supplier can provide the customer with a sample of goods for evaluation and approval at the suppliers cost and expense (clause 8.4.1). On reasonable notice the customer can also take samples of fuel at the supplier's premises to test the quality of goods being supplied (clause 8.4.5). Please note that once the fuel has been delivered to the
 
customer's tank it is very difficult to accurately verify the source of any contamination/problem were there to be one.

If following the sampling there is any disagreement as to the quality of the goods supplied by the supplier then the customer and supplier should accept the results of an agreed independent laboratory (clause 8.4.6). If it is found that that samples taken do not comply with the standards for the relevant fuel (set by HMRC) then the supplier will be responsible for covering any costs including the cleaning of affected storage tanks (clause 8.4.5).

Ordering
 
Important Note - At further competition stage suppliers tendered margins are based on two key pieces of data, the normal load size and location of the delivery point. Therefore customers should ensure that when placing orders the volume ordered corresponds with the normal load size provided to CCS on the site addition template you submitted. This was used for the further competition and is thus the load size which suppliers tendered their margins against.
